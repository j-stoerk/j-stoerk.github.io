About

I work where machine learning meets electrode manufacturing. Production lines drift: formulations change, coating and calendering setpoints move, and a model fitted on last quarter's data quietly stops being right. My research asks how a model can absorb those new process regimes without discarding what it already knows, and how far physical structure can be built into the model rather than learned from scratch.

In practice this means two threads that keep meeting: continual learning — understanding forgetting well enough to control it — and process–property modelling of electrodes, where the physics is known in outline but the closures are not.

Industrial doctorate (Dr.-Ing.) at the Institute for Particle Technology, TU Braunschweig, in the Particle Simulation & Functional Structures group of Prof. Dr.-Ing. Carsten Schilde, in cooperation with VARTA Microbattery GmbH.

Research interests

Continual learning

EWC, A-GEM, DER++ and replay-based methods; geometric accounts of interference; robust learning under shifting process conditions.

Battery electrode manufacturing

Dry electrode processing, slurry extrusion, coating, calendering, and the parameter–structure relationships that follow from them.

Electrochemical analytics

Impedance spectroscopy, cycling-data interpretation, and linking process variables to downstream cell performance.
